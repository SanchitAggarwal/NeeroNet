What is NeeroNet?

NeeroNet is an integrated and intelligent real-time water assessment model with smart analytics using machine learning and IoT to monitor water efficiently for different activities of industry, household and agriculture in urban and rural regions of developing countries by analyzing water usage and water parameters, such as pH, turbidity and contamination, at a micro level. In addition, our system will monitor rainwater harvested for groundwater replenishment when installed in channelized tanks on rooftops and alert for leakages in the distribution network when installed in the pipelines.

The Problem

India faces a big water crisis—improper management of water and natural disasters has left the country with only a limited quantity of water. In New Delhi, it is estimated that close to 40% of water is being lost to leaking pipes. In a study conducted by the Delhi Committee of the Associated Chambers of Commerce and Industry of India (ASSOCHAM), it was reported that water distribution losses are due to leakages in a network of 9,000 km-long water supply lines. Approximately 3000 million liters are pumped by government water centers but only about 1700 million liters actually reach consumers. In addition, many households go without water for extended periods of time. In fact, 27% of homes in Delhi receive tap water for less than three hours a day. The distribution loss of water in Delhi is significantly higher than the losses seen in other developing countries, where 20% of water is lost in distribution. The current gap between supply and demand of water is nearly 1,300 million liters of water. There is a demand of 4,300 million liters per day but Delhi supplies only about 3,000 million liters of water to its residents per day. This is an enormous problem for the rapidly growing population of India, which is expected to overtake China as the world’s most populous country in the world by 2030. The water distribution efficiency will need to quickly increase to meet the demands of the growing population.

Proposed Smart Meter

The purpose of NeeroNet is to develop a low cost system for real time monitoring of the water and water quality in an IoT environment. The design system applies a specialized IoT module for accessing sensor data on mobile phones and computers. The primary components of our system includes sensors (installed in pipes, water storage units, water outlet channels), BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy installed per room to receive signal from the sensors which then sends the readings to the smart meter via BLE or Wi­Fi), Raspberry Pi based smart meter which collate data from BLE modules and sends the overall home data to SmartThings Open cloud to analyze data in real­time via Wi­Fi or SIGFOX, and a web/mobile dashboard providing data visualisation for live water usage and wastage statistics as well as insights to home owners, state officials and other authorities at different levels. In summary, we are integrating existing sensors to an online platform using machine learning and IoT. We hope to eventually be able to measure both physical and chemical parameters of water, such as temperature, pH, turbidity, conductivity, dissolved oxygen concentration.

Impact

We will integrate different sensors using a Raspberry Pi controller. Water authorities can leverage our technology by utilizing the water usage and wastage statistics, track problems related to leakages and blockages in drainage system, address individual, district, city and state-wide requests, and quickly rollout new regulation and bills from central control centers based on real-time analysis. We hope to replace existing conventional water meters and roll­out Raspberry Pi based Smart Meters across different cities in India, which will allow for proper distribution of water by mapping water usage from homes to states of the country, water conservation by timely alerts for leakages, and preparedness for natural disasters. Our technology will impact industries, government agencies, and residential homes in India. We believe that the government of India is our most prominent stakeholder. NeeroNet can impact the millions of people in India who currently have limited access to poor-quality water. We hope to begin by targeting government agencies in Delhi, who have the highest stake in water. In later stages, we will specifically develop systems to apply our technology to various industries and residential homes all across India.
